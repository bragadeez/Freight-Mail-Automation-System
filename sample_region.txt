Subject: Market Update – Russia | Week 46 Trends in Ocean, Air, Rail & Multimodal Freight from
China, APAC & ISC
Dear Valued Customer,
Below is our Week 46 analytical market update for freight flows into Russia from China, the
Asia-Pacific region (APAC), and the Indian Subcontinent (ISC).
This reflects the evolving market momentum from Week 45 and early November forward
indicators.
Ocean Freight – Into Russia via Transshipment Ports (Vladivostok, St. Petersburg)
China & APAC
• Space / Capacity: Conditions continue to ease, with utilization down to 80–85% as
post–Golden Week sailings normalize. More spot flexibility emerging on South China →
Vladivostok legs.
• Delays: Vladivostok/Vostochny dwell steady at ~1 day; St. Petersburg delays
improving to 3–4 days.
• Rates / Equipment: FAK levels show mild softness (–1–2% WoW). 40 HC equipment
repositioning improving—Ningbo easing, Qingdao minor shortage.
• Premiums: Slight decline to USD 50–110/TEU as roll risks continue to reduce.
ISC (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh)
• Transit: Via Jebel Ali / Istanbul stable at 32–35 days; carriers report stronger reliability.
• Ports: Nhava Sheva & Karachi continue to operate smoothly (dwell < 1.5 days).
• Bangladesh feeders: Back to near-normal performance, with slippage < 0.5 day
expected by mid-November.
Air Freight – Into Russia (SVO, LED, DME)
China & APAC
• Rates: Stable to –1% WoW, with charters ex-PVG and CAN ahead of year-end peak.
• Capacity: Balanced across routes via DXB / IST / TBS; high reliability (5–6 days door-todoor).
• Alternatives: Trucking via Kazakhstan / Armenia performing efficiently; minimal
weather disruption (< 1 day).
ISC
• India: Exports stable; charters reduced as market steadies. Transit remains ~7 days.
• Rates: Flat to slightly softer (–0.5% WoW).
• Bangladesh: Capacity recovery reaching 90–95%; backlog clearing; rates 5–8% above
pre-incident but easing.
Rail Freight – China → Russia via Land Corridors
• Capacity: Network steady at 90–95% utilization; spot space improving gradually.
• Transit: Manzhouli ~23–24 days, Zabaikalsk ~25 days, both stable with better wagon
turnover.
• Reliability: Scheduling and uplift predictability improving; spot loads seeing fewer
holds.
• Rates: Expected to remain stable through mid-November.
Multimodal – Combined Solutions via India into Russia
• Cost / Time Efficiency: Maintains 35–40% savings vs. air and 65–70% faster than
ocean.
• Transit:
o APAC → Russia via India: 10–11 days (1 day faster WoW).
o Myanmar → Russia: 8 days, strong demand from fashion & electronics.
• Reliability: >90% on-time as MAA / DEL clear Diwali backlogs.
Political & Regulatory Factors
• Sanctions / Screening: Persistent 24–48 hr reviews at Dubai, Istanbul, and Poti for
dual-use cargo.
• China Export Controls: Documentation holds ~1.5 days for sensitive HS codes.
• Customs (India / Pakistan): Randomized inspections remain but average delay < 0.5
day.
Weather & Seasonal Conditions
• East Asia: Stable operations; occasional fog near Ningbo / Qingdao.
• South Asia: Fully normalized post-monsoon.
• Russia / Siberia: Early winter adds +3–4% fuel surcharge; minor inland trucking delays
(+0.5 day).
Advisory – Recommended Actions (Week 46)
• Ocean: Maintain 3–4-week lead time; secure 40 HC early; use premium slots
selectively.
• Air: Confirm block-space from key hubs (PVG/CAN/DAC) ahead of mid-November
rush.
• Rail: Prioritize contracted capacity to ensure consistency.
• Multimodal: Continue leveraging India-hub routes for urgent, cost-sensitive cargo; prebook transshipment windows.
• Compliance: Validate export documentation early to minimize clearance holds.
We will continue to monitor and report on any significant rate, capacity, or regulatory
developments affecting Russia-bound supply chains.
